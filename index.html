WHY ANALOG DESIGN HAS STALLED

Manual Optimization Process

Analog circuit design remains fundamentally a manual process. Engineers iteratively explore topologies, adjust device sizing, and repeatedly validate through SPICE simulation — often requiring thousands of simulations and significant time. Design space exploration is guided by expert intuition and trial-and-error.

The Design Gap

Digital design became software-driven decades ago. Analog never made that transition. Every transistor dimension, bias point, and compensation network shapes system behavior. Unlike digital synthesis, analog remains where nonlinear physics and tight constraints dominate. No off-the-shelf automation exists.

The Scale Constraint

Nearly every system powering the physical world depends on analog silicon — power management, sensing, control circuits. The global supply of analog expertise cannot keep pace with demand. Analog blocks increasingly define the critical path of chip development across industry.

OVERCOMING RECENCY BIAS

The Problem

Agentic optimization systems relying on sequential reasoning exhibit strong recency bias — generating candidates primarily from the most recent design state. When this trajectory encounters a local optimum, the system fails to recover earlier alternatives that could lead to better solutions. Single-trajectory search gets trapped.

Population-Based Solution

circuitEvolve maintains a population of candidate circuits across multiple generations, not a single trajectory. Multiple design lineages are preserved simultaneously, allowing the system to revisit earlier branches and continue exploring structural variations that initially appeared less promising.

Branch Recovery

In empirical evaluations, the final optimized circuit does not descend from the most recent iteration — it emerges from an earlier branch in the evolutionary search tree. This demonstrates the system's ability to maintain exploration across multiple design families rather than prematurely committing to a single trajectory.
